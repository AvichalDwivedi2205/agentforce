Introduction: The Burden of Unhygiene in India

India faces a substantial burden of mortality attributable to unhygienic conditions. ‚ö°

  • Inadequate sanitation, unsafe water, and poor hygiene practices.
  • Leading cause of preventable diseases, especially among children.
  • Diarrhoeal diseases are a leading cause of death for children under five.

Sanitation Access: A Persistent Challenge

Despite efforts, challenges persist in sanitation access and usage. üíß

  • Less than half the population had access to improved sanitation in 2015-16.
  • Open defecation remains a significant risk factor.
  • Initiatives like Swachh Bharat Mission have increased access, but challenges remain.

Emerging Threats & Food Safety

New dimensions of unhygiene-related mortality are emerging. üí°

  • Contamination of pharmaceuticals (e.g., cough syrups) leading to child deaths.
  • Unsafe food practices contribute to the disease burden.
  • COVID-19 highlighted the link between poor sanitation and mortality rates.
